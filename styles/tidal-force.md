# Tidal Force Diagram

**Poster as physics instrument. The system survives forces that destroy everything else.**

## Concept
A celestial body (the "66" system) experiencing tidal deformation inside the Roche limit -- the boundary where gravitational differential forces exceed internal cohesion, breaking apart most systems. The body is stretched along the gravitational axis and compressed perpendicular. Skill labels orbit at different distances: close orbit = high tidal stress (rare), far orbit = safe (common). Debris fragments outside the boundary = the remains of systems that broke apart.

## Diagram Elements
- **Tidal body:** SVG ellipse (rx:95 ry:55), white 15% stroke 1.5px
- **Tidal bulges:** Bezier protrusions on left/right, red 35%
- **Force vectors:** 4 arrows -- horizontal red (stretching), vertical white (compression)
- **Roche limit:** Dashed ellipse (rx:380 ry:250), red 30%, 2px, dasharray 14,7 + 12px blur glow
- **Equipotential lines:** 4 concentric ellipses, 3.5-8% white
- **Gravitational field lines:** 5 dashed lines from right edge, 3-5% white
- **L1/L2 Lagrange points:** Crosshair circles, 18% white
- **Debris field:** 13+ fragments -- circles (6-10% white) + elongated tidally-sheared ellipses
- **Distance scale:** Vertical gradient line + 7 ticks + labels (5R to 0 to 5R), Roche marks in red 50%
- **Measurement line:** Horizontal dashed with 2.44R label
- **Gravity source:** Arrow + label on right edge
