# Exposure Triangle Style

> Part of the [Claude Design Engine](https://github.com/rahamanujit/claude-design-engine) style library. Adapt colors and fonts to your `brand.config.json`.


## Concept
The photography exposure triangle (ISO, Aperture, Shutter Speed) as a metaphor for balancing multiple dimensions. Three scales arranged as a triangle converging on a center point = perfect exposure. Most people overexpose one dimension. The system balances all three.

## Visual Structure
- **Canvas:** 1080x1350, #0A0A0A background
- **Central artifact:** SVG triangle with thin stroke (0.12 white, 1.2px). Outer triangle + inner dashed "sweet spot" triangle. 3 medians converging on centroid.
- **Centroid:** Ring + dot in red. Stat number at convergence point with red glow.
- **Vertices:** Each labeled with a dimension (photography term + skill category). Red glow circles at vertices = overexposure warning.
- **Scale ticks:** Along each edge, 5 per side.
- **Skill labels:** Positioned along edges at 3 depth zones. Deep (red, near center), Mid (dim white), Surface (faint white, near periphery). Always horizontal, never rotated.

## Typography Rules
- **Headline dim (top):** 26px, weight 300, 18% white, 6px letter-spacing, uppercase. Wide-spaced = overexposed light bleeding. Position = top (authority zone undercut by dimness).
- **Headline bold (bottom-third):** 38px, weight 700, 92% white, -1px letter-spacing. Key word in Playfair Display italic red. Tight = focused, properly exposed. Position = grounded conclusion.
- **The weight shift 300->700 IS the exposure correction.** The letter-spacing shift +6px->-1px IS the focus pulling tight.
- **Metadata:** 7px Space Mono, 12% white, spread across bottom with photography settings.

## Instrument Elements
- **EV scale (left edge):** EV +3 to EV -3, EV 0 highlighted red.
- **f-stop scale (right edge):** f/1.4 to f/22 with gradient line.
- **Exposure meter (bottom):** Horizontal track, centered red dot = optimal, labels underexposed/optimal/overexposed.
- **Overexposure zone:** Top 60px gradient (white 3% fading down) = canvas itself is slightly blown out.
- **Corner registration marks:** 12px L-brackets, 4% white.

## Color Usage
- **Red (#C83C32):** Centroid stat, deep skill labels, key word in headline, EV 0, exposure meter optimal dot, vertex glow.
- **White (varying opacity):** Triangle edges, mid/surface skills, headline bold, scale markings.
- **Near-black:** Background, underexposure zone gradient.

## When to Use
Best for posts about balance, systems thinking, multidisciplinary approach, avoiding tunnel vision, breadth vs depth. The "overexposure" metaphor works for any "too much of one thing" narrative.
