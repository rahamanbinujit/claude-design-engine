# Social Media Design Knowledge

> What actually works on each platform. Based on real engagement data and analysis of top-performing content.

## Platform-Specific Insights

### LinkedIn

**What works:**
- Carousels: 24.42% engagement rate (4x higher than text posts at 6.67%)
- PDF carousels: 596% more engagement than plain text
- 1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1350 (portrait) formats
- Dark backgrounds for bold statement posts
- Textured/paper backgrounds for educational content
- One strong headline per image (no paragraphs)

**What doesn't work:**
- Designs that look like Facebook ads
- Corporate-looking templates with stock photos
- Perfect symmetry and polished gradients
- Multiple messages per image
- Small text (it's viewed on phones)

**Content types that perform:**
- Stats/data posts with one hero number
- Before/after comparisons
- Step-by-step breakdowns (carousels)
- Contrarian takes with bold typography
- Personal stories with authentic photos

### Instagram

**What works:**
- 1080x1350 portrait (maximum screen real estate)
- Carousel posts (10 slides max)
- Bold, scroll-stopping cover slides
- Consistent visual identity across posts
- Grid harmony (posts look good together on profile)

**What doesn't work:**
- Text-heavy slides
- Inconsistent branding between posts
- Landscape orientation (wastes screen space)
- Low contrast on mobile screens

### YouTube Thumbnails

**What works:**
- 1280x720 (16:9)
- Maximum 4-5 words
- Expressive human faces (emotion sells clicks)
- High contrast colors (yellow, red, blue backgrounds)
- Text with thick outlines/strokes for readability

**What doesn't work:**
- More than 5 words
- Small text (thumbnail appears at 120x67px)
- White backgrounds (blend with YouTube's interface)
- No face (faceless thumbnails underperform)
- Cluttered layouts

### Twitter/X

**What works:**
- 1200x675 or 1080x1080
- Dark mode friendly designs
- Clean, minimal layouts
- Large readable text

## Universal Design Principles for Social Media

### 1. Mobile First, Always
Over 80% of social media is viewed on phones. Every design decision must pass the phone test:
- Can you read the headline on a 4-inch screen?
- Is the accent color visible at small size?
- Does the layout work in the feed (surrounded by other content)?

### 2. The 3-Second Rule
You have 3 seconds to stop someone from scrolling. Your design must communicate its core message in that time. This means:
- ONE dominant visual element
- ONE clear message
- Extreme contrast between important and supporting elements

### 3. The "Looks Organic" Test
The best-performing social media designs DON'T look like designs. They look like someone's real notes, screenshots, or authentic moments. Ways to achieve this:
- Textured backgrounds (paper, grain, noise) instead of flat colors
- Handwritten or imperfect elements mixed with clean typography
- Asymmetric layouts (slightly off-grid, human)
- Small personal photo (avatar-sized, not hero)
- NO corporate color schemes or gradient overlays

### 4. Typography Hierarchy Creates Engagement
The single biggest difference between amateur and professional social media design is typography hierarchy:
- Headline: 4x bigger than body text minimum
- Weight contrast: 700 vs 300 (not 500 vs 400)
- One accent color on ONE keyword
- Tight letter-spacing on headlines, wide on labels

### 5. Whitespace = Confidence
Cramped designs scream amateur. Professional designs breathe:
- Minimum 40px padding on all sides
- Generous gaps between sections
- One idea per visual space
- When something looks wrong, add MORE space, not more elements

## Engagement Data (2026)

| Format | Avg Engagement Rate | vs Text-Only |
|---|---|---|
| Text post | 6.67% | baseline |
| Single image | ~13% | 2x |
| Carousel/PDF | 24.42% | 3.7x |
| Video | varies | 5x reach |

**Key insight:** Dwell time drives algorithm rewards. Carousels keep users engaged longer because they swipe through multiple slides.

### Carousel Best Practices
- 6-12 slides is optimal
- Engagement drops after slide 10
- Max 30 words per slide
- Hook slide determines if they swipe at all
- Include slide numbers (progress indicators encourage completion)
- Pattern interrupt on slide 2 prevents early drop-off
